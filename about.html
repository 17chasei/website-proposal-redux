About the site

The Written World is a site that seeks to teach people about writing systems and related subjects. You can expect a greater emphasis on writing systems that either particularly interest me or that are little-known.

About the author

I am Isaiah and - as you may have guessed by now - I like writing systems. I also like linguistics in general. My favorite writing systems are Dongba (along with Geba), Eskayan, Egyptian hieroglyphs (along with Demotic and Hieratic), Cherokee, and the Paleohispanic scripts. My favorite language family is Trans-New Guinea. I'm currently learning Indonesian.

Another "fun" thing I like to do is write English with other writing systems (that is, non-Latin). Here are some examples of the sentence "the quick brown fox jumped over the lazy dog" in other writing systems (according to some transliteration systems I made):

  • International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA):Still Latin, but you can use this as a reference of sorts for the other transliterations. /ðiː kwɪk braʊn fɒks dʒʌmpt oʊvər ðiː leɪzɪ dɒg/
  • Cyrillic: Ҙий куик браун фокс джымпт оувэр ҙий лейзи дог.
  • Arabic: .ذي کوک براون فاکس جمڤت اوۏر ذي ليزى داݢ
  • Hebrew: .ד׳י קוק בראון פאקס ג׳מפט אובר ד׳י לייזי דאג
  • Devanagari: धी क्विक ब्राउन फ़ॉक्स जम्प्त ओभर धी लेज़ि दॉग.
  • Phoenician: 𐤈𐤉𐤟𐤊𐤅𐤊𐤟𐤁𐤓𐤀𐤅𐤍𐤟𐤐𐤀𐤊𐤎𐤟𐤑𐤌𐤐𐤕𐤟𐤀𐤏𐤁𐤓𐤟𐤈𐤉𐤟𐤋𐤉𐤆𐤉𐤟𐤃𐤀𐤂
  • Georgian: ძი ქჳიქ ბრაჳნ ჶჸქს ჯჷმპთ ჵვჷრ ძი ლჱზი დჸგ.
