What is an abugida?

An abugida is a type of writing system that indicates vowels by modifying a base consonant letter; these modifications can be conveyed through markings around the base letter or through altering the shape or orientation of the letter. The word comes from the Ge'ez language and signifies the first four consonants of traditional abjads with the first four vowels of Ge'ezThe first four consonants (of abjads) combining with the first four vowels of Ge'ez, which give: 'ä አ, bu ቡ, gi ጊ, and da ዳ, hence 'äbugida አቡጊዳ. [1].
